Our Team

Britt

By doing this project I learned how to read from and develop off of another person’s code. In turn, I understand how to keep my code readable for others. This is a critical skill to have in software development as you are constantly building off what others have made. I also learned how to divide the components of an app into the three layers (logic, database, and UI) and how to conduct tests between those layers. For the presentation portion of iteration 3 I learned how to use a static site generator to create this website.

 
 

Kory

My major takeaway from the project was a comprehensive understanding of software testing. From the different types of tests that you run (unit,integration,system) to the code and software required to make them I gained allot of information in that field. Throughout the project I felt very comfortable writing and designing tests that would result in good code coverage and bug catching. Moving onto future endeavors I will take with me this new found skill as I am sure it will come in handy.

 
 

Liam

The project rewards me with extensive knowledge of designing whether it’s my own code or designing the structure of the project, how classes interact with each other between different layers. Document reading is also a very essential skill to have when approaching newer fields and concepts. Since not everything we do is taught in class, understanding how to look up problems in order to arrive at an optimal solution was a challenge at first and later a great skill to have. Human factor isn’t something to overlook. Working in a group develops my interaction skills with other people and thus boost our productivity.

 
 

Matt

During this Software Engineering course, I learned an invaluable set of workplace-related skills while working alongside my group-mates. First and foremost, working with my group-mates gave me a first glimpse into group-based teamwork, and the consequent communication (with the occassional compromise) that lined our work on our project. Working with non-solo project also gave me valuable experience with time management; with Iterations having being divided among group-mates and constrained under specific deadlines, I was forced to make the most of my hours while working on this project application.

 
 

Joe

I learned how to integrate with code written by others, and the importance of making clear interface guidelines and following them to avoid problems. I also learned how to better communicate what I am planning on doing, and to make sure I don’t skip over any details that will affect others’ code. Another lesson was the importance of regular communication to make sure that everyone was on the same page. Additionally, thanks to recent events, I learned that it is important to ensure we can handle sudden disruptions and changes to our development situation, such as a pandemic forcing everyone to stay home rather than meeting in person.
